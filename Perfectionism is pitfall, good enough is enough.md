# Perfectionism is pitfall

referecing: https://www.saturdaygift.com/perfectionism-quotes/



As a former perfectionist, I now think perfect is antonym of practical.



Perfectionism often make you win each battle, but lose the whole war.



some reasons:


- Perfection does not exist in reality

Perfection does only exist in theory, not really in reality.

A famous example, [trolley problem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trolley_problem), it's about how to do when there is contradictory between two fundamental values, utilitarianism vs deontology. Along well with social development, they are both essential and have their own significance. The problem is now they're contradictory.  

> “If people do not believe that mathematics is simple, it is only because they do not realize how complicated life is.”
>
> &mdash; John von Neumann

In reality, every sensible decision is made by coordinating multiple regards. You can't do one thing at any time simply according to just one theory. Sometimes maybe following the rule seems feasible, that's just because the downsides is trivial you simply overlook it. There always are some situations where the downsides become so big that you can't ignore. Then you must compromise, and complete it only in certain degree. Eventually you fail to get to 'perfection' of the theory.

For example you as utilitarianist say the minority should obey the majority, and in practice like {} you also practice it, things alright that the society or community all the time sacrifice the interests of the minority even sometimes you are of the minority. But when the sacrifice become more and more significant and you have to lose all you have without any mistake on you or you are always the minority, you begin to hesitate and doubt it, And finally you change your belif to opposite.

Here perfectionism is in fact more like an extremism of single regard.

- Perfectionists lack the big picture
Perfectionism is a certain kind of laziness, you just want to do it only once, but only iteration can make it approach perfection.

- Perfection is a notable reason of procrastination.

> People call me a perfectionist, but I'm not. I'm a rightist. I do something until it's right, and then I move on to the next thing.
>
> &mdash; James Cameron

Perfection is great difficult, or rather impossible while people start anything new such as playing games, learning a new language or do a public speech etc. This is truth, people just simply can't, not just first time, but even first fiftyth times as well. 

As perfectionist I always wanted to give a perfect performance even on my first try. And result was with no doubt I didn't get to it at all. I always felt not prepared well. So the first try was put off again and again, or sometimes I just immediately admitted I was not fit for those kinds of things and quitted at very beginning even with no try. The mindset made me always decline to get tries on a lot of things, which killed many potentional possibilities.

There is a common motto in every perfectionists: do it perfectly or not at all. So once perfectionists procrastinate, they come back to seek comfort and confidence from perfectionism: I am not ready yet for my higher standard which is the sign of great people, then perfectionists get a conclusion: the cause of procrastination is because they are of great, which makes them get further away from right path. Ironically, perfectionism, in turn, becomes excuse of procrastination.

The procrastination does not only appear before things' starting but also things' ending. Perfectionist always can't end things, feeling uncomfortable if they think there are imperfect things. They are just always busy perfecting something, getting into some certain weird situation where much time is spent but with little effect and things are all the time close to being done but never. So what does they get finally from it, only a great feeling I guess, nothing more. Here perfectionism definitely brings poor execution, Because of it, perfectionists tend not to make a plan, even though they get one, the plan is hard to be executed well.

And even worse, often some pefectionists pursue perfection not because they just simply want to be amazing perfect. It is just used as a cover for their fear. Some perfectionists severely lack self-confidence, extremly being afraid of exposing their shortcomings and making mistakes, being forced to become a perfectionist. The best way of covering their shortcoming is don't do it. Then the consequence obviously is: if you are able not to do it, don't do it, or put it off as long as possible as you can if not. Also ironically, perfectionism makes them less self-confident.

This is reminds me of the quote from Mark Twin: "It ain’t what you don’t know that gets you into trouble. It’s what you know for sure that just ain’t so".


- Perfectionism will take away your liberty

> “When things are perfect, that’s when you need to worry most.”
>
> &mdash; Drew Barrymore

Most of the time the greatest value of perfection is it brings a great feel, nothing more. But perfectionists are addicted to the feeling, perfection is like a psychotropic drug, perfectionists being the patient need to keep taking the drug to maintain the feeling. But it comes with huge price.

If you aren't a perfectionist you can opt out at any time, if you are then you have no choice except for perfecting things until it's perfect. So you are tightly bound to the imperfect things, at duration you can't focus on anything else. You instinctively refuse to switch task, even though you do, the imperfect things now become lingering annoyance. 

But if things are perfect will there be no worries? No, totally not. The long, exhausting life has just began. You have to take care of your every action to not break the perfection, you need to pay a lot of attention on the things to immediately find out when it's not imperfect, then immediately make it perfect again. If you want to maintain the feeling, you have to do it like this.

If things are perfect, perfectionists will be afraid of trying. Because if tries are being given perfectionists must proactively break the perfection. For perfectionist, breaking the perfection by themselves is very hard. They can't endure it, breaking perfect things, breaking the great feeling.

As a developer, I saw the scene many times: someone spent a lot of time pefecting a piece of code, but due to some reasons requirements changed or something else required him to delete all the code written before, this guy became quite unwilling to do it. Yeah, this is predictable, why can you casually throw it away if much hard work have been paid on something?

So why do you want your desktop tidy? You should know the feeling comes with price, sometimes the price is huge.
